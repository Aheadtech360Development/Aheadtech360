Digital Marketing for Real Estate — What Actually Works in 2026

If you've tried Facebook ads for real estate leads, you've probably hit one of two outcomes: account flagged for Fair Housing violations, or 200 leads at $4 each that never pick up the phone. Both fail for the same reason — the agency didn't understand real estate marketing.

Real estate digital marketing has three constraints most agencies ignore: Fair Housing compliance, a 3–12 month buyer decision cycle, and the hyper-local nature of the product. Miss any one of them and you're burning budget.

Meta Ads — What Fair Housing Compliance Actually Means

Meta's Special Ads Category for Housing means no age, gender, ZIP radius, or demographic targeting. Lookalike audiences use a Special Ad Audience instead. Fail to set this up and your account gets restricted — often permanently. Working within these constraints changes the strategy: behavioral interest targeting, life-transition creative, and high-intent landing pages beat demographic targeting anyway.

Google Ads — High Intent, High Cost

Searches like "homes for sale in [neighborhood]" and "real estate agent [city]" convert at far higher rates than social traffic. The problem: $8–$25 CPC in most markets, and Google broad match will burn budget on irrelevant searches without exact-match campaigns and a tight negative keyword list maintained weekly.

Real Estate SEO — The Compounding Asset

Neighborhood landing pages are the most defensible SEO play in real estate. A brokerage that ranks for "homes for sale in Williamsburg Brooklyn" generates consistent inbound at zero marginal cost — unlike ads that stop the moment budget stops. Building that requires genuine neighborhood content, GBP optimization, consistent local citations, and buyer-intent articles (market reports, neighborhood guides, school district comparisons).

Lead Nurture for the Long Cycle

Average real estate buyer takes 10+ weeks from first search to first agent contact — and another 8–12 weeks to close. A single follow-up call is not a strategy. We build email and SMS nurture sequences, market update broadcasts, and CRM automation (GHL or Follow Up Boss) that keeps you front-of-mind for the full duration of the buyer journey.

Real estate FAQ

Questions real estate professionals ask us.

Can you run Facebook Ads for real estate without violating Fair Housing rules? +
Yes — requires Meta's Special Ads Category for Housing, which restricts certain targeting (no ZIP radius, no demographic targeting). We use behavioral interest targeting and Special Ad Audiences as the compliant alternative. Getting this wrong gets accounts permanently restricted. We set it up correctly on every real estate account we touch.
What's a realistic cost per lead for real estate? +
Meta real estate leads: $8–$35 depending on market, offer, and targeting. Google Search: $25–$80 per click, 5–15% CVR on a well-built landing page. Qualified appointment cost: $40–$200 depending on market. Cheap CPL is a red flag in real estate — $3 leads rarely respond to calls.
How do you generate seller leads vs buyer leads? +
Seller leads: Meta with a home valuation hook ("what's your home worth?") — higher intent, shorter cycle. Buyer leads: Google Search for specific property type and neighborhood, Meta for pre-market awareness. The funnels are completely different and should never be combined in the same campaign.
How long does real estate SEO take? +
Neighborhood pages: 3–6 months for lower-competition terms. Map Pack movement: 2–4 months with consistent GBP work. High-competition city terms ("real estate agent NYC"): 12–24+ months. We target neighborhood-specific terms first for faster, more defensible wins.
Do you work with individual agents or only brokerages? +
Both. Minimum recommendation for agents: $1,500/month ad spend and a working website. Brokerages and teams with larger budgets handled under a full-stack retainer. Property developers get a different model — scope call required first.
Which CRM do you recommend for lead nurture? +
GoHighLevel (GHL) for full automation control. Follow Up Boss for real estate-specific features and MLS integrations. HubSpot for commercial real estate. We set up and integrate all three — GHL is our default for most agents and small teams.
Can you build real estate websites and landing pages? +
Yes — WordPress + Elementor for brokerages, GHL funnels for lead gen, and neighborhood landing page systems that scale to 20–50 pages without manual effort per page.
What makes your real estate marketing different? +
Three things: compliance-first (no account bans), lead quality focus (appointments booked — not just form fills), and full-cycle thinking (marketing doesn't stop at the form — we build the nurture that gets the appointment).

Digital Marketing for Fashion and Apparel Brands

Fashion is one of the most creative and competitive verticals in digital marketing. Everyone is running Meta Ads. Everyone has a Shopify store. The difference between brands scaling profitably and brands burning out after 6 months almost always comes down to the same three things: creative quality and testing rigor, email and retention revenue, and conversion rate on the actual site.

Meta Ads for Fashion — Angle-First Testing

The fashion vertical on Meta is crowded and CPMs are high. Standing out requires creative that earns attention immediately. Our angle-first testing framework identifies 6 emotional angles your audience responds to (identity, social proof, pain, features, humor, authenticity) and tests creatives systematically. Winners scaled in 7 days. Losers killed. After 60 days you know more about your audience than most brands learn in years.

TikTok for Fashion

For fashion brands with younger demographics (18–35) and AOV under $150, TikTok can produce CPMs 30–50% below Meta — if the creative is native enough. Polished studio content fails on TikTok. Raw, authentic UGC in the brand's aesthetic wins consistently. We source TikTok-native creators who understand fashion content.

Email Revenue — 25–35% Is Achievable

Most fashion brands earn 5–12% from email. The strong ones earn 25–35%. The difference: a Welcome Series that warms before it discounts, a 3-email Abandoned Cart with escalation structure, Post-Purchase upsell within 48 hours, and a Win-Back flow for 60-day lapsed buyers. We build all of these in 2–4 weeks.

Shopify CRO for Fashion

Fashion Shopify stores have specific conversion problems: no clear size guides showing fit on real bodies, slow mobile load killing CVR, checkout flow asking for too much too soon. Our CRO audits for fashion stores find 8–14 conversion blockers per site on average — most fixable in under 2 weeks.

Fashion FAQ

Questions fashion brands ask us.

How much should a fashion brand spend on Meta Ads? +
Minimum $3,000/month to collect enough data to optimize. At $5,000/month you can run a proper angle-testing system. Under $3K, campaigns stay in learning loops without enough purchase signals to exit them.
Is TikTok better than Meta for fashion? +
Depends on three things: customer age (TikTok skews 18–30), AOV (works well up to $120–$150), and creative capacity (you need native TikTok-style content, not studio ads). When all three fit, TikTok often produces 30–50% better CPMs than Meta. Luxury or $200+ AOV brands — Meta is still stronger.
How do I fix low conversion rate on my fashion Shopify store? +
Most common culprits: size guide hard to find or doesn't show fit on real people, mobile load over 3 seconds, no social proof visible before scroll on product pages, and single-button checkout not enabled. These four alone typically lift CVR 15–30%.
